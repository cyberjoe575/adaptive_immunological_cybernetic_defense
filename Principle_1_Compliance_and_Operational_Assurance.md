# Principle 1  
## Compliance and Approval Are Foundational Starting Conditions, Not Operational Assurance

Compliance, approved configurations, and governance controls establish foundational operational intent and known-good starting conditions. However, they do not independently establish operational assurance or ongoing trustworthiness.

Operational environments continuously evolve due to changing behaviors, relationships, dependencies, environmental conditions, threats, and operational context. As a result, operational assurance can never be considered complete, static, or permanently achieved.

Trust is not binary or permanent. Trust is continuously evaluated, behaviorally influenced, contextually dependent, and subject to degradation or restoration over time.

Operational assurance must therefore emerge from the continuous correlation and validation of multiple signals, including:
- foundational compliance,
- runtime behavior,
- relationship integrity,
- environmental context,
- identity consistency,
- control effectiveness,
- and human-governed investigation and validation.

Assuming that compliant or approved systems are inherently secure or trustworthy creates dangerous complacency and provides adversaries opportunities to exploit static trust assumptions as concealment for malicious activity.

What traditional security models treated as the goal — compliance and approval — becomes the starting point for continuously validated operational assurance.
