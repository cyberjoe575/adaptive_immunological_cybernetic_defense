# Principle 2  
## Trust Must Be Continuously Evaluated

Trust is not a static, binary, or permanent condition. Trust is not a fire-and-forget state.

Operational trustworthiness cannot be established through a single validation event, approval process, compliance assessment, or point-in-time security review. What is trusted today may not remain trustworthy tomorrow, and what is not trustworthy today may become trustworthy again through investigation, validation, and operational change.

Trust must therefore be continuously synthesized from multiple correlated operational signals, including:
- foundational compliance,
- runtime behavior,
- relationship integrity,
- communication patterns,
- temporal activity,
- identity consistency,
- privilege usage,
- environmental conditions,
- threat intelligence,
- approved operational intent,
- and validation outcomes.

Trust evaluation must operate continuously across the enterprise through a unified operational trust abstraction layer that synthesizes trust signals into near real-time operational assurance and human decision support.

Abnormalities are not inherently malicious, but they always require evaluation to determine root cause. Abnormal behavior may indicate:
- malicious activity,
- operational instability,
- environmental changes,
- dependency failures,
- evolving business operations,
- or legitimate changes in expected behavior.

Only root-cause analysis can determine whether abnormalities require:
- mitigation,
- increased guardrails,
- isolation,
- trust restoration,
- or incorporation into evolving operational norms.

“What is” establishes current operational trust norms. “What is to be” establishes authorized future-state trust expectations. Trust evaluation must continuously reconcile approved operational intent with observed operational reality in order to distinguish malicious abnormality from legitimate operational evolution.

Trust assumptions must continuously be revalidated because, in the era of AI-assisted vulnerability discovery and machine-speed exploit development, static trust assumptions create concealment opportunities for undetected compromise, including within fully compliant and approved systems.

Uncertainty itself is a trust-degrading condition. When operational confidence becomes unclear, scrutiny, observability, contextual evaluation, and human investigation should increase proportionally until confidence can be restored, constrained, or reevaluated.

The systems responsible for synthesizing trust and operational assurance become crown-jewel infrastructure themselves and must therefore remain continuously validated, difficult to discover, difficult to persist against, and difficult to corrupt without detection.

Trust must therefore remain continuously evaluated, contextually informed, behaviorally influenced, operationally adaptive, recursively validated, and human-governed throughout the operational lifecycle.
